What words trigger a positive or negative response in the chat filter in pubg?

My Data

I used simulated chat data created in a harvard experiment in which a chatbot was used to create simulated statements based on real statements said in the game PUBG. The full dataset can be found here

My Analysis

After Filtering through each word and removing some filler words, I was able to determine that amount of times a word is associated with a positive or negative response from a chat filter
The sentiment analysis of PUBG chat data reveals intriguing patterns in player expressions. Notably, phrases like "smoke bombs" and "victory within" emerge as positively associated, suggesting their frequent use in favorable contexts. Conversely, terms such as "lost cause," "chicken dinner," and "smoke grenade" carry a negative sentiment, possibly reflecting frustration or challenges in the game. Interestingly, phrases like "feels like" and "enemy squad" appear neutral despite varying usage frequency. This analysis sheds light on the nuanced sentiments embedded in PUBG chat, providing valuable insights for community engagement and game experience enhancement.

My Answer

Generally positive words such as "Victory" and "win" were always associated with a positive response from the filter. Other words that may not have an inherently negative meaning but are most oftenly used more negativley than positivley such as "need" and "chance" were most oftenly associated with a negative response from the chat filter
