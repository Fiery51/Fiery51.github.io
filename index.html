How do our words help contribute to our sentences being positive or negative?

Imagine you're deep in a video game, with the clock counting down and the pressure mounting. You and your teammates are working frantically to clinch that last-minute win. In these critical moments, communication is your most powerful ally—until a single misguided message sows the seeds of toxicity and team morale begins to crumble.

For anyone who's ventured into the world of online gaming, toxicity is all too familier. It can be found everywhere within online communities and games and can sour the game's spirit, transforming fun into annoyance. Many of us play to escape and to enjoy, but negativity has a way of breaking the virtual experience. Today, we're going to delve into the intricacies of our online communcations, examining how our choice of words and phrases can shape our sentences to be perceived as positive or negative.

Looking at our data...

After Filtering through each word and removing some filler words, I was able to determine that amount of times a word is associated with a positive or negative response from a chat filter. The sentiment analysis of PUBG chat data reveals intriguing patterns in player expressions. Notably, phrases like "smoke bombs" and "victory within" emerge as positively associated, suggesting their frequent use in favorable contexts. Conversely, terms such as "lost cause," and "smoke grenade" carry a negative sentiment, possibly reflecting frustration or challenges in the game. Interestingly, phrases like "feels like" and "enemy squad" appear neutral despite varying usage frequency, of which we will look at later.

As we go from this first group of data to our 2nd its good to look at what this data means. These words are generated from a bot, not too unlike chat-gpt, but instead trained on real chat data from in game sentences said by real players. It gives us a peek of how positive or negative messages and phrases might pop up throughout our everyday language without us even thinking about it.

Looking at our 2nd graph...

Looking at our 2nd graph of our chat data, we find that another intresting fact about how we communicate with others online. This graph shows how through our specific word choices, it impacts our sentences positively or negatively. Words such as "Victory" and "Win" were almost always associated with positive values.

Another interesting observation that comes along with this graph is the analysis of generally nuetral words. Despite their generally netrual nature with words such as "chance" or "attempt" can, in the moment of an intense combat situation, be tinted with more negativity than positivity. This can be due to a multitude of reasons that aren't apparent in the graph but the most likely is an underlying tone and context of the situation that a graph like this just can't articulate.

In the end...

The words we choose in our daily and online communications matter and do more than just convey basic information. This analysis of generated chat data has been able to make a connection between specific word choices and how those sentences might be percieved by others as positive or negative. For instance, words commonly associated with success, such as "victory" and "win", consistently create more positive sentences. These words are overall positive and celebratory in their nature and create better environments around us.

The words we use have the power to be able to influence the mood and moral of teamates, coworkers, and friends around usand can impact the overall tone of an interaction with someone. Our word choices have a subtle but major impact on our language and it shows that every word we choose serves to foster relations with others we talk with.
